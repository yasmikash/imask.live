Literature Survey

The current epidemic is spreading rapidly because the public has no rights way to determine and prevent COVID-19. Outside of conventional laboratory tests, COVID-19 monitoring is low. Since the Covid-19 virus began to spread around the world, Researchers began to search for optimal solutions using IoT and it stopped spreading Early detection of viruses and virus-infected individuals. Here are some of the Research done.

In [01], Petrovic and Koci’c developed a solution to indoor safety using IoT. The solution measures the user’s body temperature, detects whether the user wears a face mask, and assesses the social distance is kept practicing. The system used contactless methods to approach the desired functionalities. Another IoT-related solution proposed by Otoom et.al.[02] for early identification of the virus using real-time data collected through a wearable device. The device gathers data on symptoms such as cough, shortness of breath, fatigue, spo2, fever, sore throat, and several sensors. And the study used several machine learning algorithms to make accurate predictions about the individuals. Scientists from NTU and A*STAR [03] have developed an integrated monitoring system that can be easily attached to any face mask in order to monitor the wearer for health indicators associated with Covid-19. Sensors pick up skin temperature, blood oxygen saturation, blood pressure and heart rate – all of which are parameters associated with coronaviruses.

Research Problem

The COVID-19 pandemic is an ongoing health crisis that the world is facing nowadays, and wearing masks is a key measure to curb COVID-19 from rapidly spreading. According to the World Health Organization, COVID-19 had infected over 82 million people and killed more than 1.8 million worldwide as of 31st December 2020. While COVID-19 patients can be accurately determined using Rapid Antigen and PCR tests, these tests are costly, time-consuming, invasive, and uncomfortable. It is important to identify patients at an early stage as a proper treatment for COVID-19 has not yet been found. Therefore, these currently available tests are inadequate to continuously monitor the COVID-19 suspects and this prompted need of active research in developing smart solutions to identify and track COVID-19 suspects.

Proposed Solution

The iMask is a wearable IoT-based intelligent mask that identifies and tracks COVID-19 suspects in real-time. This is achieved by embedding smart sensors into the wearable face mask and analyzing the data gathered using the smart sensors using Artificial Intelligence models. The proposed intelligent mask gathers real-time data related to five major symptoms of COVID-19: body temperature, cough, heart rate, breathing pattern, and blood oxygen level. Gathered data are used to predict COVID-19 suspects, while the location tracking feature enables the tracking of the COVID19 suspects.

Research Objectives

Identifying and analyzing the temperature

The main objective is to identify people with high body temperature. Fever is one of the most common symptoms among COVID-19 patients. If the temperature is high, the person is more likely to have a fever. Therefore, obtaining the body temperature is a valuable aspect in detecting possible COVID-19 suspects. When the person wears the mask, the temperature sensor detects the body temperature as an array of data and passes it into the process and gets the average value.

Identifying and analyzing cough rate

The main purpose is to identify people with cough and calculate the cough rate of COVID-19 patients using audio processing. When a person wears the mask and coughs, the sensor gets an audio clip with data using the microphone and sends it to the process, and then identifies whther the audio consists of cough, if audio consists of cough, then determine the cough rate.

Identifying and analyzing the breathing pattern

The main purpose is to identify people with shortness of breath, as difficulty in breathing is one of the common symptoms of COVID-19. Therefore, the mask constantly fetches the respiratory rate to be processed and analyzed by the system.

Identifying and analyzing heart rate

The main purpose is to identify the heart rate of the person who is wearing the mask. When the person wears the mask, the heart rate sensor detects the heart pulse as an array of data, and passes it into the process, and calculates the average heart rate as the output. The mask can also detect that the person is wearing the mask even if the mask is switched on using the array of the data.

Identifying and analyzing blood oxygen saturation level

The main purpose is to Identify the blood oxygen saturation level of who is wearing the mask. When the person is wearing the mask and breathing, the mask identifies blood oxygen saturation level by the pulse oximeter sensor and then processes the data set and gets the percentage of blood saturation level.

Identifying and analyzing location

The main purpose is to identify the location where people travel with a mask on or without a mask but carrying their smartphone instead. Location data can be fetched from the GPS module installed into the mask or the GPS services provided by the smartphone. In either case, the data is collected to report and analyze the COVID-19 positive individuals in a certain area, but the privacy of the user is always guaranteed to be protected by the system.

Methodology

High Level System Diagram

Figure 1: Layers of iMask

Figure 2: iMask Prototype

The proposed intelligent mask is composed of three layers. The outer layer is the outer cover of the mask which is made of plastic. The Middle layer is also made of plastic and acts as a space for the filter, i.e., N-95 filtering. The filter can be mounted between the outer layer and the middle layer. The inner layer is made of silicone and rubber. All the sensors are attached to this inner layer, hence, this mask fits well on the face. The Figure 1 illustrate the three layers of the iMask and the locations of the sensors mounted in the iMask respectively.


The proposed intelligent mask and system consists of 6 main components. They are.

  • 1. Body Temperature Detection
  • 2. Respiratory Rate Detection
  • 3. Cough Detection & Cough Event Identification
  • 4. Blood Oxygen Saturation Level Detection
  • 5. COVID-19 Suspect Detection
  • 6. Location Tracking


As illustrated in Figure 2, sensors embedded into the masks are used to collect data related to five major symptoms of COVID-19, body temperature, cough, heart rate, breathing pattern, and blood oxygen level. All the sensor data are collected in real-time and saved in the Azure cloud database. Note that the mask does not have an Internet connection, hence, these data are uploaded to the cloud platform when the mask is connected to the mobile phone using Bluetooth.

Data uploaded to the cloud are sent to the backend server, where the backend services and AI models are deployed. These data undergo the analysis processes, as explained below, to get accurate severity of the symptoms. The cough is identified using VGG-16 neural network model using transfer learning, while the cough rate is generated with the help of convolutional recurrent neural network (CRNN). The heart rate and the respiratory rate are identified using two LSTM models, and blood oxygen saturation level and the accurate body temperature are determined using the multiple linear regression and XGBoost models, respectively. Finally, the outcome of each symptom is fed into the final AI model to predict whether the individual is a COVID-19 suspect or not.
